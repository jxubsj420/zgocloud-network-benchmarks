## What Does "China Optimized VPS" Actually Mean?

Before diving into ZgoCloud's catalog, let's pin down what "China optimized" means in practice—because it's a term that gets thrown around a lot, and not all optimization is created equal.

Standard international VPS traffic between the US West Coast and mainland China typically routes through congested public peering points. Result? Packet loss, jitter, and latency that can spike to 300ms or worse during peak hours.

A genuinely China-optimized VPS uses **premium transit**—dedicated, less-congested paths that bypass the usual bottlenecks:

- **CN2 GIA (Global Internet Access)** — China Telecom's top-tier international network. Low latency, minimal packet loss, and the gold standard for China-bound traffic. If you're serving China Telecom users, this is what you want.

- **AS9929 (China Unicom Premium)** — China Unicom's answer to CN2 GIA. Dedicated premium routing that avoids Unicom's congested 4837 backbone. Excellent for Unicom users.

- **CMIN2 (China Mobile International Network 2)** — China Mobile's premium international path. If your audience skews mobile-heavy (and whose doesn't in China?), CMIN2 keeps things snappy.

The best China-optimized providers combine **multiple** premium routes so that traffic takes the optimal path regardless of which Chinese carrier the end user is on. ZgoCloud does exactly this with their top-tier plans, running GIA + 9929 + CMIN2 simultaneously.
